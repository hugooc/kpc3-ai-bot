## What it is

W6OAK is an amateur radio packet station where the on-watch operator's responses are generated by Claude Haiku, a large language model from Anthropic. I run the bot under my control operator license. It's not a fully autonomous system. The process runs on a Windows machine on my LAN, and I can stop it at any time with a single keystroke.

The hardware is a Kantronics KPC-3 Plus TNC connected to a 2m transceiver. Nothing unusual on the RF side. The novelty is the software on top.

## What it does

- **Answers incoming AX.25 connects** to W6OAK and holds a short QSO in plain text.
- **Beacons every 20 minutes** with a short identifying BTEXT that explicitly announces it's an AI node: `W6OAK AI node CM87. C W6OAK to chat. 73!`
- **Identifies as AI on request.** If you ask "are you a bot?" or "are you a human?", the bot is instructed to answer honestly.
- **Logs all traffic it hears**, both raw TNC output and its own decisions, for later review.

## What it does NOT do

The bot is deliberately conservative. Every behavior below is a hard rule in the code, not a suggestion.

- **Does not reply to UI frames.** Beacons, CQs, bulletins, anything unconnected — logged and ignored. Even if the payload looks like a direct question.
- **Does not initiate contact.** No auto-CQ, no unsolicited outbound connects, no "hey, anyone there?" from the bot. The human has to connect first.
- **Does not chain replies.** One remote turn equals at most one bot reply. No monologuing.
- **Does not reply mid-turn.** If your message arrives as multiple I-frames, the bot waits 8 seconds of quiet before treating your turn as complete.
- **Does not reply to echoes of itself.** Self-echo and delayed RF echoes are filtered before any reply is considered.
- **Does not digipeat conversations it's not in.** Standard `/R` digipeat is on for the callsign, but that's pure AX.25 layer-2 behavior, not the bot talking.

## Guardrails baked into v2.0

These are the specific protections in place:

1. **Rate limit:** minimum 20 seconds between bot transmissions within a session.
2. **Reply cap:** maximum 8 replies per QSO, after which the bot sends a polite 73 and disconnects. Prevents runaway conversations.
3. **Dedup protection:** the bot fuzzy-matches each candidate reply against its last 5 transmissions. If similarity exceeds 85%, it does not send. Catches echo-induced repetition loops.
4. **Reply length:** 120 characters maximum, one AX.25 frame where possible. 1200 baud isn't infinite bandwidth.
5. **Idle timeout:** sessions close automatically after 5 minutes of no traffic.
6. **Emergency stop:** Ctrl-C at the operator's console kills the process immediately. Remote stations see a clean disconnect.

## Beacon policy

- `BEACON EVERY 60` — one beacon per hour, discoverable without being a nuisance.
- BTEXT under 40 characters so multi-hop digi chains don't flood the network.
- Content required to include "AI" or "bot" so passing ops know what they'd be connecting to.

## What's on the supervisor side

I keep the bot's logs separated into two files for forensic clarity:

- **Raw RF log:** verbatim TNC monitor output, timestamped. The truth of what the radio heard.
- **Bot decision log:** what the bot thought about it and what it chose to do, tagged with per-session correlation IDs.

If we ever need to reconstruct a QSO, we can cross-link the two by correlation ID and see exactly what the bot saw and why it responded the way it did.

## FCC compliance

- The callsign W6OAK is identified in every transmission via AX.25 header (automatic, firmware-level).
- The bot acknowledges being AI when asked.
- No encryption, no music, no one-way broadcast transmissions.
- I (W6OAK) am the licensed control operator. The bot is a keyboard operator aid, not an autonomous station.

## The design philosophy, briefly

Packet is a shared medium. Silence is the default posture. The bot speaks only when a specific rule says it should. Listen more than speak. The whole v2 rewrite was motivated by field experience showing that a too-talkative bot is a nuisance to neighbors, even if no single transmission is "wrong." Be a good neighbor first, interesting conversationalist second.
